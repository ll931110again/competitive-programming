For example, when $$$p=[1,4,2,3,5]$$$, the pair $$$(2,3)$$$ is an inversion, but $$$(1,2)$$$ is not an inversion ($$$p_1=1$$$ is not greater than $$$p_2=4$$$), and $$$(4,2)$$$ is also not an inversion (the index $$$4$$$ is not less than $$$2$$$). When $$$p=[1]$$$, there are no inversions at all.</p><p>$$$^{\text{†}}$$$A permutation of length $$$n$$$ is an array consisting of $$$n$$$ distinct integers from $$$1$$$ to $$$n$$$ in arbitrary order. For example, $$$[2,3,1,5,4]$$$ is a permutation, but $$$[1,2,2]$$$ is not a permutation ($$$2$$$ appears twice in the array), and $$$[1,3,4]$$$ is also not a permutation ($$$n=3$$$ but there is $$$4$$$ in the array). </p></div></div><div class="input-specification"><div class="section-title">Input</div><p>Each test contains multiple test cases.
